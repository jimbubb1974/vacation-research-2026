Greece: Athens, Naxos & Santorini

Ancient ruins at first light, ferry crossings through the Aegean, sandy beach days, whitewashed island towns, and a caldera finale that feels unmistakably Greek.

Why this one works

This is the big, iconic trip. Athens brings the ancient-world weight, Naxos gives the route room to breathe, and Santorini finishes with the view everyone already has in their head.

The structure is deliberate: two Athens nights, three Naxos nights, three Santorini nights, then one Athens buffer before the flight home. July heat is real, so the best days start early, pause in the middle, and come back alive at sunset.

Days 2-4

Athens

Page 2
Photos: The Parthenon and Anafiotika, Athens, via Wikimedia Commons.

The opening scene

Athens is the arrival point, but it should not feel like a layover. The first day stays light: airport train, hotel check-in, Plaka, Anafiotika, and a rooftop dinner with the Acropolis lit above the city.

The second morning is the moment: timed Acropolis entry right at opening, before the heat and tour groups take over. The museum and a shaded lunch carry the day through the hottest hours.

Jul 12Arrive ATH, settle in, Plaka and Anafiotika walk, rooftop dinner with Acropolis view.
Jul 13Acropolis at 8 am, Acropolis Museum, shaded lunch, siesta, food tour or Monastiraki evening.

Designed around the heat

July in Athens can push 95-104°F. That does not ruin the city, but it changes the rhythm: archaeology early, museum or shade midday, streets and dinner after the temperature drops.

The next move is also part of the experience. A conventional Blue Star ferry from Piraeus to Naxos is slower than the fast boats, but steadier in summer winds and easier to enjoy as a real Aegean crossing.

Jul 14Morning ferry from Piraeus to Naxos, arrive early afternoon, swim at Agios Georgios, sunset at the Portara.

The vibe

Athens gives the trip its depth right away. It is hot, layered, a little chaotic, and exactly where this itinerary should begin.

Days 4-7

Naxos

Page 3

The island that makes the trip breathe

Naxos is the right middle act because it is beautiful without being over-tight. It has Chora, beaches, mountain villages, the Portara, and enough space to feel like an island rather than a stage set.

The Mount Zas morning gives the trip an active spine: start before the heat, pass Zeus's Cave, then loop through Filoti, Apeiranthos, Halki, and the citron distillery for the village side of the island.

Jul 14Arrive by ferry, beach reset, Portara sunset, dinner in Chora.
Jul 15Mount Zas at dawn, Filoti lunch, Apeiranthos marble village, Halki and citron tasting.

Beach day, boat day, easy evening

The second full day can go two ways: catamaran and snorkeling if winds cooperate, or a simpler beach day at Plaka or Agia Anna if the meltemi is up.

That flexibility is the point. Naxos carries the adventure and recovery parts of the trip at the same time, which makes the faster Santorini finale feel earned rather than rushed.

Jul 16Half-day catamaran, snorkeling, beach club afternoon, waterfront dinner in Naxos Town.
Jul 17Late swim, lunch in Chora, fast ferry to Santorini, Skaros Rock sunset walk.

Why this stop matters

Naxos keeps Greece from becoming only heat, crowds, and logistics. It gives the route room, texture, beaches, villages, and a few days where the schedule can loosen.

Days 7-11

Santorini Finale

Page 4
Photo: Oia, Santorini at sunset, via Wikimedia Commons.

The view everyone came for

Santorini is more intense than Naxos, which is why it belongs at the end. By the time the ferry pulls in, the family is already in island mode and ready for the caldera drama.

The best day starts early on the Fira-to-Oia hike. Ten kilometers along the rim is a real experience, especially before the heat rises and before Oia turns into the afternoon version of itself.

Jul 17Arrive Santorini, check into Imerovigli or Fira, Skaros Rock sunset walk.
Jul 18Fira-to-Oia hike at 6:30 am, Oia lunch, bus or taxi back, quieter caldera dinner.

Ancient-world depth

Acropolis, Acropolis Museum, Akrotiri, and the sense that the trip is connected to something older than the scenery.

Island movement

Ferries, harbors, beach towns, village roads, boat days, and the shift from Naxos ease to Santorini spectacle.

Heat-smart rhythm

Early starts, midday shade, beach or pool pauses, and evening walks make July workable instead of punishing.

Protected ending

The final Athens buffer night keeps the international flight away from ferry and island-delay risk.

The pitch

This is the most iconic option: not the easiest, not the quietest, but the one with ancient Athens, real island time, and the Santorini finish. It is the big trip for a reason.

Jul 19Caldera catamaran with volcano, hot springs, and Red Beach; Akrotiri by afternoon; quiet viewpoint dinner.
