Days 2-5

Munich + Neuschwanstein

Page 2
Photos: Nymphenburg Palace and Neuschwanstein Castle, via Wikimedia Commons.

Munich gets enough time

The first days are built to settle in without wasting the city: Marienplatz, Viktualienmarkt, Eisbach surfers, Deutsches Museum, BMW Welt, beer gardens, and a slower palace day at Nymphenburg.

Four nights means Munich is not just an arrival point. It becomes a proper base with enough room for museums, markets, gardens, and a no-rush first European rhythm.

Jul 12Arrive Munich, Marienplatz, Viktualienmarkt, Englischer Garten, beer garden dinner.
Jul 13Deutsches Museum, BMW Welt and BMW Museum, Hofbräuhaus or neighborhood beer garden.
Jul 15Nymphenburg Palace, Olympic Park, Schwabing or Maximilianstrasse.

The castle day

Neuschwanstein is the obvious visual anchor: regional train to Füssen, bus up toward the castles, timed interior tour, Marienbrücke bridge view, and enough Alpine backdrop to make the long day feel worth it.

It does require early ticket discipline. July timed entries sell out, so the interior tour is one of the first things to book once the trip direction is set.

Jul 14Train to Füssen, Neuschwanstein timed tour, Marienbrücke view, optional Hohenschwangau, return to Munich.

The vibe

This opening act is polished and recognizable: city energy, big museums, palace gardens, beer gardens, and the most famous castle day in Bavaria.

Days 6-8

Berchtesgaden

Page 3
Photo: Obersee, Bavaria, Germany, via Wikimedia Commons.

Lake and mountain drama

Berchtesgaden keeps the Alpine scenery in the trip without turning the whole itinerary into a hiking plan. The Königssee boat is the clean centerpiece: electric boats, echo off the cliffs, St. Bartholomä, and clear mountain water.

The town itself gives the route a quieter pause between Munich and Salzburg: old streets, local dinners, and a good night before the history day.

Jul 16Train from Munich to Berchtesgaden, old town walk, Royal Palace square, local Gasthof dinner.
Jul 17First Königssee boat to St. Bartholomä, return by boat, afternoon rest or town walk.

History plus the salt mine

The next day carries the serious history: Documentation Center Obersalzberg, Eagle's Nest if weather allows, then the Berchtesgaden salt mine before crossing into Salzburg by Bus 840.

The salt mine adds variety without another mountain day: underground history, tunnels, slides, and a practical rainproof activity if the ridge weather is not cooperating.

Jul 18Documentation Center, Eagle's Nest, Berchtesgaden salt mine, Watzmann Express bus to Salzburg.

Why this stretch works

It gives the trip visual Alps, meaningful history, and a memorable underground activity in a compact two-night stop.

Days 8-11

Salzburg + Hallstatt

A three-night finale

Salzburg is compact enough to enjoy without grinding everyone down: fortress funicular, Mirabell Gardens, Getreidegasse, Mozart's Birthplace, cafe time, and St. Peter Stiftskeller if the reservation lines up.

The third night changes the Salzburg stay. It creates room for Hallstatt as a true day trip instead of forcing Salzburg to be only a brief finale.

Jul 18Arrive Salzburg from Berchtesgaden, check in, light dinner and Old Town evening.
Jul 19Hohensalzburg Fortress, Mirabell Gardens, Mozart's Birthplace, Getreidegasse, St. Peter dinner.

Hallstatt day

Lake village, mountain backdrop, boat crossing, salt history, Beinhaus, and a return to Salzburg by evening.

Big landmarks
