Bavaria + Salzburg

Four distinct worlds in one trip: Munich energy, painted Alpine villages, glass-clear mountain lakes, and a Salzburg finale that feels like stepping onto a film set.

Why this one works

This is the balanced trip: active without being punishing, European without being hot and hectic, varied without needing a car. Every move is by train or bus, every base earns its nights, and the itinerary keeps weather-sensitive mountain days flexible.

Think science museum and river surfers, painted Alpine houses, slot canyons and cable cars, a silent electric boat across Konigssee, a serious history day at Eagle's Nest, then Salzburg's fortress, gardens, cafes, and old streets.

Days 2-6

Munich to Mittenwald

Page 2
Photos: Marienplatz and New City Hall, and Mittenwald with Karwendel Alps, via Wikimedia Commons.

The trip starts with city energy

Munich is the soft landing: arrive, ride the S-Bahn into town, get outside, eat well, and let everyone adjust to Europe time without wasting the first day.

It is also an easy place to find memorable stops: Eisbach river surfers in the Englischer Garten, BMW Welt, the BMW Museum, and the Deutsches Museum with its U-boat, mine, aviation, space, and technology exhibits.

Jul 12Marienplatz, Viktualienmarkt, Glockenspiel, Eisbach surfers, beer garden dinner.
Jul 13Deutsches Museum, BMW Welt, BMW Museum, Hofbrauhaus or neighborhood beer garden.

Then the Alps show up

Mittenwald is the visual pivot: painted houses, mountain walls, violin-making history, lake swims, and easy rail access to the big Alpine day trips.

The best part is flexibility. If Zugspitze is clear, go high. If it is clouded in, Leutaschklamm and the lakes near Mittenwald still make the day feel like a win.

Jul 14Train to Mittenwald, painted old town, Geigenbaumuseum, optional Karwendelbahn sunset.
Jul 15Garmisch day trip: Partnach Gorge, Alpspitzbahn panorama, summer luge option.
Jul 16Zugspitze if clear; Leutaschklamm, Lautersee, or Ferchensee if weather says stay lower.

The vibe

Munich gives the trip momentum. Mittenwald gives it the "we are really in the Alps now" feeling, without forcing the route into a car-dependent mountain lodge version of the trip.

Days 7-9

Berchtesgaden

Page 3
Photo: Obersee, Bavaria, Germany, via Wikimedia Commons.

The lake day is the showstopper

Konigssee is not a normal lake stop. The morning starts with a short bus ride, then a quiet electric boat between steep green walls to St. Bartholoma and onward toward Salet.

The payoff is Obersee: a short walk to a glacial lake that looks almost unreal, especially before the crowds build. This is the day that makes the photo album.

Jul 17Mittenwald to Berchtesgaden by regional rail. Longest luggage day, then old town and early dinner.
Jul 18First boat to St. Bartholoma and Salet, hike to Obersee, optional salt mine if rain moves in.

A serious history day, then Austria

Eagle's Nest is worth doing because it combines spectacular mountain access with real historical context. The Documentation Center first keeps it grounded, then the Kehlstein bus and elevator take the group to the ridge.

By evening, the trip changes countries almost casually: Bus 840, the Watzmann Express, carries everyone from Berchtesgaden into Salzburg in under an hour.

Jul 19Documentation Center Obersalzberg, Eagle's Nest if weather allows, lunch with Watzmann views, bus to Salzburg.

Why this stretch stands out

Boat echo, glacial lake, mountain tunnels, wartime history, and a cross-border bus ride in one 48-hour stretch. It feels active and cinematic without being overbuilt.

Days 9-11

Salzburg Finale

Photo: Aerial view of Salzburg old town, via Wikimedia Commons.

A compact, beautiful ending

Salzburg is the low-friction finale: walkable old streets, fortress views, Mirabell Gardens, cafes, Mozart sites, and dinner in a city that feels polished without needing a packed schedule.

It also gives the trip a clean emotional arc. Start in Munich, climb into the Alps, spend two days in Berchtesgaden's mountains and history, then finish in a baroque city with the Alps still visible behind it.

Jul 19Arrive from Berchtesgaden, check in, evening old town stroll.
Jul 20Hohensalzburg Fortress, Mirabell Gardens, Mozart's Birthplace, Getreidegasse, St. Peter Stiftskeller dinner.
Jul 21Early Railjet to Munich, S-Bahn to MUC, fly home.

City energy

Munich museums, old squares, markets, beer gardens, BMW, and Salzburg's old town.

Mountain days

Partnach Gorge, Zugspitze or Leutaschklamm, Konigssee, Obersee, and Alpine lake swims.

Clean logistics

Trains and buses do the work. No rental car, no island ferries, no extreme July heat.

Built-in breathing room

Enough structure to feel substantial, enough backup options to keep weather from taking over.

The pitch

This is the trip that gives you Europe, mountains, history, water, food, and downtime in one clean route. It is not the flashiest option. It is the one most likely to feel good every day.
